upma

Upma is a savory, South Indian breakfast dish made from roasted semolina (rava/suji) cooked with spices, vegetables, and sometimes lentils. It's a quick, easy, and filling meal, often served with coconut chutney or pickle.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup semolina (rava/suji)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, etc.)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2-3 cups water
  • Coriander leaves for garnish
  • Optional: lemon juice for tanginess

Instructions

  1. Dry roast the semolina in a pan until light golden. Set aside.
  2. In the same pan, heat oil or ghee. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add urad dal and chana dal, sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add chopped green chilies and onions, sauté until onions are translucent.
  5. Add mixed vegetables and sauté for a few minutes.
  6. Pour in water and add salt. Bring to a boil.
  7. Gradually add the roasted semolina, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  8. Cook on low heat until the semolina absorbs the water and becomes fluffy.
  9. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with coconut chutney or pickle.

Tips

  • Adjust the water quantity based on the desired consistency.
  • You can add nuts like cashews for extra crunch.
  • For a richer flavor, use ghee instead of oil.
  • Experiment with different vegetables based on availability.
  • Adding a squeeze of lemon juice enhances the flavor.

Variations

  • Vegetable Upma: Add more vegetables like bell peppers, corn, or spinach.
  • Masala Upma: Include spices like turmeric, cumin, or garam masala for added flavor.
  • Rawa Upma: Use coarse semolina for a different texture.
  • Upma with Coconut: Mix in grated coconut for a richer taste.
  • Upma with Poha: Combine semolina with flattened rice (poha) for a unique twist.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve with coconut chutney or tomato chutney.
  • Pair with pickle for an extra kick.
  • Enjoy with a side of yogurt for a cooling effect.
  • Can be served as a light lunch or dinner option.
  • Goes well with a cup of tea or coffee.

Storage

Upma is best served fresh, but leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Reheat before serving. It can also be frozen for longer storage, but the texture may change upon reheating.

Nutrition

Upma is a nutritious dish, providing carbohydrates from semolina, fiber from vegetables, and protein from lentils. It is low in calories and can be a healthy breakfast option when prepared with minimal oil.

Conclusion

Upma is a versatile and comforting dish that can be customized to suit individual tastes. Whether enjoyed for breakfast or as a snack, it remains a beloved staple in South Indian cuisine. Its simplicity and quick preparation make it a favorite among many households.

FAQs

  • Can I make upma without vegetables? Yes, you can make plain upma without vegetables, but adding them enhances the flavor and nutrition.
  • Is upma gluten-free? No, traditional upma made with semolina contains gluten. However, you can use gluten-free alternatives like rice flour or millet flour.
  • Can I prepare upma in advance? Yes, you can prepare it in advance and reheat it when needed. It tastes best when fresh, though.
  • What can I substitute for semolina? You can use coarse rice flour or millet flour as a substitute for semolina.
  • How spicy can I make upma? You can adjust the spice level by adding more or fewer green chilies according to your preference.
  • Can I add protein to upma? Yes, you can add cooked lentils, peas, or even scrambled eggs for extra protein.
  • Is upma suitable for vegetarians? Yes, upma is a vegetarian dish, but ensure that the ingredients used are vegetarian-friendly.
  • Can I make upma in a microwave? Yes, you can prepare upma in a microwave by following similar steps, adjusting the water and cooking time accordingly.
  • How long does it take to cook upma? The entire process takes about 20-30 minutes, including preparation and cooking time.
  • Can I make upma spicy? Yes, you can adjust the spice level by adding more green chilies or spices like red chili powder.
